Setting Up Back Tap

Back Tap is what lets you play a sound by double- or triple-tapping the back of your iPhone, even from the lock screen. To set it up:

  1. Open TapToot once so iOS registers the "Play Toot" shortcut.
  2. Open the Settings app.
  3. Tap Accessibility.
  4. Tap Touch.
  5. Scroll to the bottom and tap Back Tap.
  6. Choose Double Tap or Triple Tap.
  7. Under "Shortcuts," select Play Toot.

The Setup tab inside TapToot walks you through the same steps with on-screen instructions.

Common Issues

Back Tap fires but no sound plays

Make sure your iPhone isn't on silent (the ring/silent switch on the side). TapToot plays through the ringer audio channel, so silent mode will mute it.

"Play Toot" doesn't show up under Back Tap shortcuts

Open TapToot once and let it fully load, then close it. iOS only registers App Intents after the app has run at least once. Re-check the Back Tap settings list — it should appear.

Music or a podcast pauses when a toot plays

This is intentional. While a sound plays, TapToot briefly takes over your iPhone's audio focus so the toot is guaranteed to come through. Audio from other apps resumes as soon as the toot finishes.

A recording I imported won't play

Some audio formats aren't supported by iOS playback. If a file fails to play, try converting it to .m4a in Voice Memos or another audio tool, then re-importing.

Microphone access denied

If you tapped "Don't Allow" the first time TapToot asked, re-enable it in Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone → TapToot.
