Aside Support

Last updated: May 5, 2026

Aside is a hands-free voice-capture utility for iPhone. Tap your phone's back, press the Action Button, or say "Hey Siri" — Aside records, transcribes on-device, and turns the result into a summary, class notes, brainstorm, or to-do list. A separate Sounds mode lets you fire designed chimes or your own recordings the same hands-free way.

If something isn't working, the answer is probably below. If not, email us — we read every message.

Setting Up a Hands-Free Trigger

Aside ships an App Shortcut named Toggle Voice Note that any of these surfaces can run. Set up whichever one you prefer (or several):

Back Tap (any iPhone 8 or later)

  1. Open the Settings app.
  2. Tap Accessibility → Touch.
  3. Scroll to the bottom and tap Back Tap.
  4. Choose Double Tap or Triple Tap.
  5. Under "Shortcuts," select Toggle Voice Note.

Action Button (iPhone 15 Pro and later)

  1. Open the Settings app.
  2. Tap Action Button.
  3. Swipe to the Shortcut option.
  4. Tap Choose a Shortcut → under Aside, select Toggle Voice Note.

Siri

Say "Hey Siri, Toggle Voice Note with Aside." To customize the phrase, open the Shortcuts app, tap the Toggle Voice Note shortcut, and edit it.

AssistiveTouch and Voice Control

Both are configured under Settings → Accessibility. The Setup tab inside Aside walks through each one with on-screen instructions.

Common Issues

The trigger fires but nothing seems to happen

Aside records in the background and saves the note when you trigger it again. Open the app, tap the Notes tab, and pull-to-refresh — your note should be at the top. If you see a "Recording…" banner that won't clear, fully quit Aside (swipe up from the app switcher) and reopen it.

"Toggle Voice Note" doesn't show up in the Shortcuts list

Open Aside once and let it fully load, then close it. iOS only registers App Intents after the app has run at least once. Re-check the Shortcuts list — it should appear.

The Summary / To-Do / Class Notes chips are grayed out

AI formats are generated by Apple Intelligence, which requires an iPhone 15 Pro, 15 Pro Max, or any iPhone 16 or later, plus Apple Intelligence enabled in Settings. Aside shows the reason inline when the feature is unavailable. Transcription still works on every supported iPhone.

"Send to Reminders" doesn't do anything

The first time you tap it, iOS asks for Reminders access. If you tapped "Don't Allow," re-enable it in Settings → Privacy & Security → Reminders → Aside. Aside only writes the parsed checklist items; it never reads your existing reminders.

Recording sounds quiet or cuts out with AirPods

Aside enables both Bluetooth audio profiles for the mic, but switching between AirPods and the built-in mic mid-recording can drop a fraction of a second. If audio quality is poor, force the route by manually selecting your input in Control Center before triggering the note.

Music or a podcast pauses when I record or play a sound

This is intentional. While Aside records or plays a soundboard tone, it briefly takes over your iPhone's audio focus so the recording isn't drowned out and the sound is guaranteed to come through. Audio from other apps resumes as soon as Aside is done.

An imported sound won't play

Some audio formats aren't supported by iOS playback. If a file fails to play, try converting it to .m4a in Voice Memos or another audio tool, then re-importing.

Microphone or Speech Recognition access denied

If you tapped "Don't Allow" the first time Aside asked, re-enable the permission in Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone (or Speech Recognition) → Aside.

Privacy

Aside collects nothing and sends nothing off your device. Voice, transcripts, and AI formatting are processed entirely on-device. Read the full policy: Privacy Policy.
